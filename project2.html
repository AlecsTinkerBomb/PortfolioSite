Overview

One semester, three parts.

This semester consisted of one large project split into three parts: Modular Mass, Tectonic Assemblies, and A Place to Dwell.

P1: Modular Mass explored massing through the aggregation of cubes, investigating light interaction and the relationship between solid and void. The end result was a small single-person dwelling.

P2: Tectonic Assemblies developed a truss frame system that shaded and defined the exterior space of the dwelling. The project's goal was to clearly articulate the boundary between interior and exterior.

P3: A Place to Dwell situated the dwelling and frame system within an artist's retreat on the Fayetteville site of Monte Ne. Program designation and site circulation were the critical focus.
